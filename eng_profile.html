Research Content

The research focuses on estimation of speech recognition results. While recent advancements in speech recognition technology have been remarkable, there is no guarantee of 100% accuracy due to the inherent nature of the recognition mechanism. In everyday speech recognition, errors are observed compared to readings, and this poses challenges for developers using existing recognition systems for app development. Hence, there is a need for mechanisms to evaluate the reliability of recognition results. If the recognition results are deemed unreliable, consideration should be given to how they are used in the app. Despite efforts to improve speech recognition accuracy, the packaging of speech recognition systems as black boxes in recent years has made it difficult to easily improve them. To address this issue, the research hypothesizes that input speech causing recognition errors is more ambiguous and unstable in time compared to clear pronunciations, and proposes to estimate the reliability of recognition results by evaluating the stability of speech. By subjecting input speech to distortion processing to the extent that it does not affect stability, the change in speech recognition results can be observed to estimate reliability.
